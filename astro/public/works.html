Problem Setting
SZU
Logo
01
Clinical Goal
Accurate and temporally consistent segmentation of the left ventricle (LV) from echocardiography videos to estimate the ejection fraction ($LV_{EF}$).
02
Imaging Challenges
Severe speckle noise and rapid non-rigid deformations cause blurred anatomical contours across the cardiac cycle.
03
Technical Bottleneck (Rank Collapse)
Existing linear recurrent models rely on unconstrained Euclidean state updates. Over long sequences, this causes progressive singular value decay in the state matrix.
3
Method: OSU
SZU
Logo
Orthogonalized State Update (OSU)
To prevent rank collapse, we constrain the memory evolution on the Stiefel Manifold:
$$\mathcal{V}_{C_{v},C_{k}}=\{S\in\mathbb{R}^{C_{v}\times C_{k}}:S^{\top}S=I_{C_{k}}\}$$
$$S_{t}=\mathrm{Proj}_{\mathcal{V}}(S_{t}^{\mathrm{Euc}})$$
1.
High-order Newton-Schulz Iteration: Efficient projection without expensive SVD computation.
2.
Geometric Stability: Preserves fine-grained valvular motion across temporal transitions.
4
Method: APFE
SZU
Logo
Anatomical Prior-aware Feature Enhancement
A physics-driven process explicitly separates anatomical structures from speckle noise:
1.
Acoustic Bias Estimation
$$M_{t}=AvgPool_{K\times K}(X_{t})$$
2.
Polarity-aware Decoupling
$$X_{t}^{+}=ReLU(X_{t}-M_{t}), \quad X_{t}^{-}=ReLU(M_{t}-X_{t})$$
3.
$X_{t}^{+}$ isolates high-frequency edges (myocardium), while $X_{t}^{+}$ captures low-response regions (blood pools).

Experiments
SZU
Logo
01
SOTA Performance
Evaluated on CAMUS and EchoNet-Dynamic. OSA achieves 94.82 mDice on CAMUS, surpassing GDKVM (94.18).
02
Real-time Efficiency
Achieves an inference speed of 35 FPS, satisfying real-time clinical deployment requirements.
03
Geometric Regularization Validated
OSU strictly forces SVVar = 0.00 and significantly minimizes Temporal Drift, ensuring rigorous temporal evolution.
